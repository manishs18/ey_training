Planner Architecture: Distinguishing LTM and STM for Annual Budgeting

Definitions

LTM (Long-Term Memory) - Stable information that persists across
budgeting cycles. - Strategic objectives, historical spending, policies,
constraints, and institutional knowledge. - Updated infrequently.

STM (Short-Term Memory) - Working memory used during the current
budgeting exercise. - Current-year assumptions, calculations,
negotiations, drafts, and intermediate decisions. - Exists only for the
duration of the planning session.

1.  Commute / Transportation

LTM - Previous 10 years of transportation budgets - National mobility
strategy - Existing highway, rail, and metro commitments - Historical
traffic growth trends - Fuel consumption patterns - Cost benchmarks for
road construction - Multi-year infrastructure projects

STM - Current year’s traffic projections - New metro proposal under
evaluation - Inflation assumptions - Current contractor bids - Budget
gap calculations - Draft allocation scenarios

2.  Defence

LTM - National security doctrine - Multi-year procurement programs -
Fleet modernization roadmap - Historical readiness metrics - Existing
contracts - Strategic threat models

STM - Current geopolitical developments - Procurement requests for this
cycle - Current exchange rates - Budget trade-off analysis - Readiness
calculations

3.  Agriculture

LTM - Historical crop yield databases - Irrigation coverage statistics -
Farmer subsidy policies - Long-term food security goals - Climate
patterns - Previous program effectiveness

STM - Current monsoon forecast - Expected crop demand - Regional drought
alerts - This year’s subsidy proposals - Cost-benefit analysis

4.  Government / Public Administration

LTM - Constitutional spending obligations - Pension liabilities - Tax
collection history - Fiscal responsibility rules - Multi-year economic
plans - Department spending baselines

STM - Current GDP forecast - Revenue estimates - Deficit calculations -
Cabinet recommendations - Draft budget versions

Planner Architecture View

Planner ├── LTM │ ├── Historical Policies │ ├── Strategies │ └──
Constraints └── STM ├── Current Year Assumptions ├── Simulations └──
Draft Plans

Rule of Thumb

LTM answers: “What do we already know and what commitments must be
honored?”

STM answers: “What is happening this year and what allocation options
are we currently evaluating?”

A planner retrieves strategic context from LTM, loads relevant facts
into STM, performs reasoning and optimization in STM, then optionally
writes finalized decisions back to LTM for future budget cycles.
