AI ORCHESTRATION PATTERN SELECTION – PART B

================================================== 1. INSURANCE – Claims
Adjudication ==================================================

Selected Pattern: GraphFlow

Why? • Fraud screening, policy coverage check, and medical coding review
are independent. • All three run in parallel. • Final decision depends
on all three outputs. • GraphFlow is designed for parallel execution
with dependency management.

Runner-up: Magentic Why Runner-up? • Supports multiple agents but is
more flexible than required.

Why NOT other patterns? • Round-robin: Executes agents one after
another, not parallel. • Selector: Chooses only one agent instead of
combining multiple results. • Swarm/Handoff: Focuses on transferring
work between agents rather than parallel execution. • Magentic: Can work
but is less structured than GraphFlow for DAG workflows.

================================================== 2. RETAIL – Buyer’s
Research Assistant ==================================================

Selected Pattern: Magentic

Why? • Number of tasks is unknown beforehand. • Agent dynamically plans
research. • Can invoke web search, supplier lookup and trend analysis. •
Produces a consolidated report.

Runner-up: Swarm/Handoff Why Runner-up? • Multiple agents collaborate
but planning is less autonomous.

Why NOT other patterns? • Round-robin: Fixed sequence is inefficient for
dynamic research. • Selector: Picks one agent instead of coordinating
many tasks. • GraphFlow: Requires predefined workflow while research is
unpredictable. • Swarm/Handoff: Good collaboration but lacks Magentic’s
planning capability.

================================================== 3. MANUFACTURING –
RFP Response Builder ==================================================

Selected Pattern: GraphFlow

Why? • Workflow follows a defined sequence. • Reviewer may send sections
back for rework. • Supports conditional branches and feedback loops.

Runner-up: Selector Why Runner-up? • Can assign specialists but cannot
naturally model review loops.

Why NOT other patterns? • Round-robin: Cannot efficiently handle
conditional rework. • Swarm/Handoff: Better for ownership transfer than
structured workflows. • Magentic: Too dynamic for a predictable business
process. • Selector: Only selects specialists and lacks complete
workflow orchestration.
