OBSERVABILITY & GOVERNANCE: THE ULTIMATE TRACE VS. AUDIT LOG CHEAT SHEET
================================================================================

This document provides a comprehensive guide to understanding the differences between 
Distributed Traces/Spans and Audit Logs, along with step-by-step solutions to the 
MeridianPay platform routing assignment.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. CORE CONCEPT COMPARISON
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

AXIS               | TRACE / SPANS 📊                        | AUDIT LOG 📜
-------------------|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------
Core Question      | How did it run? Where's the time?       | Who did what? Prove it.
Completeness       | Sampled — keeps ~10% (transient)        | Every record — never sampled (100%)
Mutability         | Expires, can be dropped                 | Append-only, tamper-evident
Lifetime           | Hours -> weeks                          | Months -> years (WORM: Write Once, Read Many)
Audience           | SRE, Developers, FinOps                 | Compliance, Legal, Security
Built from         | OpenTelemetry spans                    | Signed / hash-chained sink

KEY RULE OF THUMB:
- Choose TRACE if the task asks WHERE, WHY, HOW FAST, or HOW MUCH.
- Choose AUDIT LOG if the task asks WHO, PROVE, KEEP, or ALL OF THEM.
- Choose BOTH if you need structural timing flow across 100% of un-sampled records.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. ASSIGNMENT TICKETS & GUIDED SOLUTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TICKET 01: SRE On-Call
Prompt: "p99 latency on the underwriting agent jumped to 4.2s overnight. Find which sub-step ate the time."
👉 CORRECT ANSWER: Trace / Spans
💡 Why: It deals directly with performance metrics ("latency", "4.2s") and requires breaking down internal execution into individual blocks ("which sub-step ate the time") to find timing bottlenecks.

TICKET 02: Regulator
Prompt: "A regulator demands: who approved loan #88213, on what model version, with what inputs — and proof it was never altered."
👉 CORRECT ANSWER: Audit log
💡 Why: Submitted by a Regulator focusing on compliance ("who", "what inputs"). It explicitly demands "proof it was never altered," which is only fulfilled by the permanent, cryptographic, append-only nature of a hash-chained Audit Log.

TICKET 03: Compliance
Prompt: "Prove the PII-redaction guardrail ran before the model call on all 12,400 approvals last quarter."
👉 CORRECT ANSWER: Audit log
💡 Why: 
1. "On all 12,400 approvals" requires the Audit Log because traces are sampled at 10% (which would drop 90% of the data).
2. "Ran before the model call" requires a Trace to inspect the structural, parent-child timing flow hierarchy to prove execution order.
Therefore, you must check the Audit Log to ensure zero missing records, and use the 'trace_id' to jump into the trace to verify sequential alignment.

TICKET 04: SRE On-Call
Prompt: "You're reproducing a single failing request in staging — show the full call tree and pinpoint where it errored."
👉 CORRECT ANSWER: Trace / Spans
💡 Why: It asks for an execution diagram ("full call tree") to inspect internal software boundaries for a "single failing request" in an engineering environment ("staging"). No compliance or historical tracking is needed.

TICKET 05: FinOps
Prompt: "FinOps wants average cost & tokens per request on a live dashboard. Sampling 10% is fine with them."
👉 CORRECT ANSWER: Trace / Spans
💡 Why: FinOps is looking for aggregated trends ("average cost & tokens") to power a "live dashboard." The ticket explicitly validates that "Sampling 10% is fine," matching your platform's 10% trace sampling architecture.
